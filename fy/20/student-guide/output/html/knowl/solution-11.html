( Instructor solution preview: show the student solution after due date. )SOLUTION If the function is exponential, its formula is of the form \(y=ab^x\text{.}\) The points (-2,18) and (1,2/3) are on the graph so, \(18 = ab^{-2}\)\(\frac{2}{3} = ab^{1}\) Taking the ratio of the second equation to the first one we have \(\frac{2/3}{18} = \frac{ab^1}{ab^-2}\)\(\frac{1}{27} = b^3\)\(b = \frac{1}{3}\text{.}\) Substituting this value of b into the second equation gives \(\frac{2}{3} = a \left( \frac{1}{3} \right)^1\)\(a = 2\) Thus, \(y = 2 \left( \frac{1}{3} \right)^x\text{.}\)
