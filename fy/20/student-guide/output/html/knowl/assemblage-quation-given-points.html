Determine Equation of a Line Given Two Points.

Let \((-2,3)\) and \((1,7)\) be points on a line. If we find the slope, we may use the same process as the previous assemblage to find the equation. The slope is:

\begin{gather*} m = \dfrac{7-3}{1-(-2)} =\dfrac{4}{3}. \end{gather*}

We now know half of what we need for the equation: \(y = \frac{4}{3}x + b\text{,}\) where \(b\) is the \(y\)-intercept. Both points are on the line and so if we pick a point and plug in the \(x\) and \(y\) values, we can find \(b\text{:}\)

\begin{align*} 7 & = \frac{4}{3}(1) + b\\ 7 - \frac{4}{3} & = b\\ \frac{21}{3} - \frac{4}{3} & = b\\ \mbox{So, } b & = \frac{17}{3} \end{align*}

Hence, the equation is \(y = \frac{4}{3}x + \frac{17}{3}.\checkmark\)
