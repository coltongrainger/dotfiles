The defining aspect of a vertical line is the fact that all of it's \(x\)=coordinates are the same. In this case, the \(x\) values of all the points on the line are \(-2.\) So, the equation for the line is \(x = -2,\) since it tells all we need to know about “where” the line is and what it looks like.\(\checkmark\)
