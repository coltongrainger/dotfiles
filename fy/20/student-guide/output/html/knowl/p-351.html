Suppose we attempt to solve these two equations by elimination. One approach is to multiply the first equation by \(2\) and subtract the second equation. The result is:

\begin{align*} 4x + 6y &= 16\\ -\underline{(4x + 6y}&\underline{\, = -3)}\\ 0 \amp = 19 \end{align*}

Hey, what happened? \(0\) does not equal \(19\text{,}\) no matter what \(x\) is. Mathematically, we see that these two equations have no simultaneous solution. You asked the question “When will both of these equations be true?” And the math answered, “Hey, buddy, not until \(0\) equals \(19\text{.}\)” So our conclusion must be: \(\text{No solution}.\checkmark\)
