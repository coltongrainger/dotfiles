Determine Slope Given Two Points.

Determine the slope of the line passing through the points \((5, 0)\) and \((5, 3).\)

We again calculate the rise and divide by the run. This time we let \((5, 0)\) be the "first" point and \((5, 3)\) be the "second" point:

\begin{gather*} m = \frac{y_2 - y_1}{x_2 - x_1}=\frac{3 - 0}{5- 5} \text{ Oops, we are in trouble here!} \end{gather*}

Since \(5 - 5 = 0\) and we cannot divide by zero, the slope is not defined. This is a vertical line where all the points have an \(x\)-coordinate of \(5\text{,}\) i.e. the line passes vertically through \(x = 5\) on the \(x\)-axis.\(\checkmark\)
