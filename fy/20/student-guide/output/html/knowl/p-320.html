Now consider the equation \(y = 2\sqrt{x}\text{.}\) How many \((x, y)\) pairs are there that satisfy this equation? Answer: \((0, 0)\text{,}\) \((1, 2)\text{,}\) \((4, 4)\text{,}\) and \((9, 6)\) are all solutions; and there is an infinite number of other solutions. (And don't forget non-integer solutions, such as \((\frac{1}{4}, 1)\text{!}\))
