GENERAL RULE FOR THE SPECIAL CASES: If you solve an equation and get a mathematical impossibility such as \(0 = 13\text{,}\) there is no solution. If you get a mathematical tautology such as \(0 = 0\text{,}\) there are an infinite number of solutions and you should find an expression for the solutions in terms of \(x\text{.}\) For example, \(\left(x, \frac{2}{3}x+\frac{8}{3} \right)\text{,}\) where the \(y\) coordinate is replaced by \(mx+b\text{,}\) the equation for the line. Remember \(mx+b\) equals \(y\text{.}\) When you express \(y\) in terms of \(x\text{,}\) someone can plug in any value for the \(x\)-coordinate and determine the corresponding \(y\)-coordinate on the line.
