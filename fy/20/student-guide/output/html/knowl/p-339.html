Why does substitution work? We found in the first step that \(y = 2x - 8\text{.}\) This means that \(y\) and \(2x - 8\) are equal in the sense that we discussed in the first chapter on functions-they will always be the same number, in these equations-they are the same. This gives us permission to simply replace one with the other, which is what we do in the second (“substitution”) step.
