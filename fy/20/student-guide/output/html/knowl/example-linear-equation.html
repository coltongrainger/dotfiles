Example 1.39. Determine Slope and Intercept from Linear Equation.

Any equation that can be written in slope-intercept form is the equation for a line. So, \(2x-5y = 15\) represents a line since:

\begin{align*} 2x-5y & = 15\\ -5y & = -2x+15\\ y & = \frac{2}{5}x-3. \end{align*}

We see that the \(y\)-intercept of this line is at \(y = -3\) and the slope is \(\frac{2}{5}.\checkmark\)
