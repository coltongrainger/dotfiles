The slope of a line may be calculated from any two points on the line, \((x_1, y_1)\) and \((x_2, y_2)\text{.}\) The "rise" is found by finding the difference between the two \(y\) values, \(y_2 - y_1\) and dividing by the "run", the difference in the \(x\) values: \(x_2 - x_1\text{.}\) Note that you must find the differences in the same order. You may not calculate the change from \(y_1\) to \(y_2\) and then the change from \(x_2\) to \(x_1\text{.}\) So the formula for finding the slope of a line from two points on the line is:

\begin{gather} \text{Slope through two points }(x_1, y_1) \text{ and } ( x_2, y_2) = \dfrac{y_2 - y_1}{x_2 - x_1}\tag{1.9} \end{gather}
