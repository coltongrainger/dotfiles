Determine Slope Given Two Points.

Determine the slope of the line passing through the points \((3, 2)\) and \((-1, 2).\)

Calculate the rise and divide by the run. This time we let \((3, 2)\) be the "first" point and \((-1, 2)\) be the "second" point:

\begin{gather*} m = \frac{y_2 - y_1}{x_2 - x_1}=\frac{2 - 2}{-1- 3} = \frac{0}{-4} = 0 \end{gather*}

Since the slope is zero, the line is horizontal with all \(y\)-coordinates equal to \(2.\checkmark\)
