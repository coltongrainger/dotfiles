Let's consider the two equations from the graph above:

\begin{align*} y &= 2\sqrt{x}\\ y &= x + \frac{1}{2} \end{align*}

Taking our estimated solution of \(3\text{,}\) if you plug the number \(3\) into both of these functions, will you get the same answer?

\begin{align*} 3 \rightarrow 2\sqrt{x} &\rightarrow 2\sqrt{3} \approx 3.46\\ 3 \rightarrow x + \frac{1}{2} &\rightarrow 3.5 \end{align*}

Pretty close! Similarly, \(2\sqrt{.1} \approx 0.632\text{,}\) which is quite close to \(.1+\frac{1}{2} = 0.6\text{.}\) But if we want more exact answers, we will need to draw a much more exact graph, which becomes very time-consuming.
