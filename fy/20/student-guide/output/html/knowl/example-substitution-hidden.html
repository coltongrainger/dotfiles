Solve the following system of equations by substitution.

\begin{align*} 3x + 4y &= 1\\ 2x - y &= 8 \end{align*}

  1. The easiest variable to solve for here is the \(y\) in the second equation.

    \begin{align*} 2x - y &= 8\\ -y &= -2x + 8\\ y &= 2x - 8 \end{align*}

  2. Now, we plug \(2x - 8\) in for \(y\) in the other equation:

    \begin{gather*} 3x + 4 (2x - 8) = 1 \end{gather*}

  3. We now have an equation with only \(x\) in it, so we can solve for \(x\text{:}\)

    \begin{align*} 3x + 8x - 32 &= 1\\ 11x &= 33\\ x &= 3 \end{align*}

  4. Finally, we take the equation from step 1, \(y = 2x - 8\text{,}\) and use it to find \(y\text{:}\)

    \begin{gather*} y = 2 (3) - 8 = -2 \end{gather*}

    So \((3,-2)\) is the solution. You can confirm this by plugging this point into both of the original equations, and getting true statements (or two true equations).\(\checkmark\)
