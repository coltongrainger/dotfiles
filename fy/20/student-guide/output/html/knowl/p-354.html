Let's solve the first equation for \(x\text{:}\)

\begin{align*} 2x + 3y & = 8\\ 2x & = 8 - 3y\\ x & = 4 - \frac{3}{2} y \end{align*}

Substituting the expression for \(x\) into the second equation:

\begin{align*} 4(4 - \frac{3}{2} y) + 6y \amp = -3 \amp\amp\\ 16 - 6y + 6y \amp = -3 \amp\amp \\ 16 \amp = -3 \amp\amp \text{It happened again!!!} \end{align*}

Even though the numbers are different between the two methods, our conclusion must remain the same: \(\text{No solution}.\checkmark\)
